A visionary disruptor who redefined fashion through innovation and rebellion.

Rei Kawakubo was born on October 11, 1942, in Tokyo, Japan. She studied fine arts and literature at Keio University before embarking on her fashion career.

In 1969, she founded Comme des Garçons, a brand that would soon become synonymous with avant-garde fashion and groundbreaking design.

She opened her first boutique in Tokyo in 1975, exclusively designing women's clothing that challenged conventional aesthetics.

In 1981, Rei Kawakubo made her Paris Fashion Week debut, captivating the fashion world with her unique, deconstructed pieces.

Three years later, she established a boutique in Paris, solidifying Comme des Garçons' presence in the global fashion scene.

Rei's designs are known for their anti-fashion ethos, characterized by austere, avant-garde, and often deconstructed garments.

In 2004, she launched Dover Street Market, a concept store that blends high fashion with a unique, curated shopping experience.

Rei Kawakubo continues to influence and redefine fashion, pushing boundaries and inspiring designers worldwide with her fearless creativity.
